You are welcome to use generative AI tools when contributing to Soteria, as we acknowledge their usefulness and use them ourselves.
However, Soteria is a soundness-sensitive codebase, we enforce an AI policy:
- We ask that you have either written, or read and reviewed yourself, **every part** of your contribution. You are responsible for its quality.
- If a significant portion of your code, PR description, review comments or messages has been AI-generated, this **must be disclosed**, stating what kind of tool was used and for what.
- **The human-in-the-loop must fully understand all code.** If you can't explain what your changes do and how they interact with the greater system without the aid of AI tools, do not contribute to this project.
- However the code was written, the person submitting the contribution must sign our [CLA](./CLA.md).
- In issues and PR discussions, **we expect to talk to a human**. Be prepared to engage during contribution review. Reviewers expect to discuss the changes with you directly.
- PRs submitted by autonomous AI agents roaming the internet will be rejected.

_The above policy is inspired and composed of several existing AI policies, including that of [Ghostty](https://github.com/ghostty-org/ghostty/blob/main/AI_POLICY.md) and [OCaml](https://github.com/ocaml/ocaml/blob/trunk/AI.md)_
